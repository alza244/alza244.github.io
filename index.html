PythonPizza, order pizza with python!

Published May 13, 2014

Pizza.. pizza... pizza.. pizaa.......my mouth is drooling..

I made a website where you can order a Domino's small cheese pizza within the click of a button. As for why I made this, well, I'm just bored. :)

Just click a button, wait a few minutes... TADA! This project is based in Python using the Dominos API.

DISCLAIMER: If you're using my pizza website for the first time, it's going to ask for your address and your card info. If the "card info" part sounds a bit scary to you (it should)!, try using a card you don't really care about, like a Cash App card.

BlueBot, an "AI" Java chatbot

Published May 13, 2014

Chatbots. It's the new, trendy thing for people to use nowadays, from generating a resume to creating company names. So yeah, they're a pretty big deal.

So, in my first semester of college, I made a scripted Java chatbot named BlueBot (creative name, huh?). It's one of the most basic forms of chatbots, which detects a word or key phrase by the user and returns the appropriate response. Basically a bunch of if-then statements.

BlueBot can also express emotions, like happiness, anger, sadness, etc. Apparently, I didn't do a good job at designing the emotions, because pretty much everyone that I've shown this too couldn't take its emotions seriously because they were "too cute". Robots have emotions too, you know!

Conclusion: You can try it out here if you'd like, I'm also going to make it open source just so more people could have fun with it. (probably some kid stealing this for his homework.. steal away, I don’t mind!)
